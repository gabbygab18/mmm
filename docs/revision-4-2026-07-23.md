# Revision 4 — 23 July 2026 (hero gap fix)

## The problem

Revision 2 fixed the heroes by rendering each composed banner at its natural
aspect ratio. That solved the cropped-out photos, but over-corrected: the
banners are roughly 1.2:1, so on a 1440px-wide screen the hero rendered about
1200px tall. The result was an enormous empty band of blue before the next
section — About was over 4000px tall, and the FAQ hero was a near-empty screen.

## The fix

The section height is now capped and the banner is cropped with `object-cover`
instead of dictating the height.

The crop is anchored to the top of each asset, which is where the photograph
actually lives. Measured from the artwork by isolating warm (non-blue) pixels:

| Banner | Photo occupies | Hero height (desktop) |
|---|---|---|
| About | rows 0–410 of 917 | 540px |
| Why Music Matters | rows 0–350 of 802 | 500px |
| FAQ | rows 0–227 of 790 | 360px |
| Contact | no photo — wave only | 420px |

At 1440px wide the cover scale is 1.309, so a 540px band shows source rows
0–412 — the whole photograph stays in frame with the subject centred. Mobile
heights are 250–300px, where the banner is short enough that almost all of it
shows anyway.

The light-streak wave sits at the *bottom* of each source asset and gets cropped
away, so it is layered back in as a separate element using the existing
`streak.png`. That is actually better than having it baked in: it now bleeds
past the section edge into the band below, which is what the mockups show.
