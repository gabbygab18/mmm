# Revision 9 — 23 July 2026 (streak overlay)

## What was wrong

Your inspector showed it exactly: the streak element measured **1280 × 922**.

`streak.png` is a 1099 × 792 canvas that is mostly empty — the actual wave only
occupies rows 313–663. Rendered at `w-full`, that whole canvas scaled with the
viewport, so on a 1280px screen it became a 922px-tall transparent sheet lying
over the entire hero *and* about 330px of the section below it. Combined with
the `landing-wave-glow` drop-shadow, which applies to the element as a whole,
that produced the wash over everything — the faded "Explore the Science and
Impact of Music" heading in your screenshot is the same overlay.

## Two fixes

**1. The asset is now the wave, not a canvas.** `streak-band.png` is
`streak.png` cropped to rows 313–663 — 1099 × 350 instead of 1099 × 792. Mean
alpha 27% rather than a sheet of near-empty pixels.

**2. Height is pinned, not width-driven.** Sizing by width alone still made the
band 78% of a tall hero and *taller than* a short one (FAQ came out 408px on a
376px band). It is now `h-[32%]` of the band with `object-cover object-center`,
so the wave keeps the same visual weight regardless of hero height, and the
crop keeps its bright core.

The `landing-wave-glow` filter is gone — it was the main source of the haze.
`waveOffset` is gone too; the band is positioned by its own half-height
(`translate-y-1/2`), which straddles the boundary the way your arrow indicated.

## Tuning

One value: `h-[32%]` in `page-hero.tsx`. Raise it for a heavier wave, lower it
for a subtler one. `translate-y-1/2` controls how much sits below the boundary —
`translate-y-1/3` would tuck more of it inside the hero.
